About the Memorial

The Kigali Genocide Memorial is the final resting place of more than 250,000 victims of the 1994 Rwandan Genocide against the Tutsi. It serves as both a burial site and a museum dedicated to educating visitors about the genocide and Rwanda's journey toward reconciliation and healing.

Managed by the Aegis Trust, the memorial opened in 2004 on the 10th anniversary of the genocide. It is one of the most important historical sites in Africa.

Duration Allow 2–3 hours for a full visit
Photography Permitted in gardens; not inside the museum
Dress Code Respectful, modest clothing required

What to Expect During Your Visit

  1. Welcome and orientation at the entrance
  2. Walk through the permanent exhibition on the history of Rwanda
  3. The genocide exhibition — a deeply moving account of 1994
  4. The children's memorial — a tribute to the youngest victims
  5. African genocide exhibition — broader context across the continent
  6. The memorial gardens and mass graves
  7. Reflection space and book of remembrance

Tips for Visitors

  • Be prepared emotionally — the content is deeply moving
  • Speak quietly and respectfully throughout the site
  • Counselors are available on-site if needed
  • Do not take photos inside the museum galleries
  • Leave a donation to support the memorial's work
  • Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds
